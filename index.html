Abstract: Watermarking is commonly used to verify the authenticity and authorship of content, serving as a means to protect intellectual property rights. In this paper, we introduce a vocoder regeneration attack that can effectively remove imperceptible watermarks embedded in audio. Our method first converts the audio into mel-spectrogram representation and then reconstructs it. This flexible approach can be instantiated using various existing audio synthesis models. We show that the watermark is vulnerable to such attack. Despite successfully eliminating the watermark, the process does not degrade the audio's quality or introduce distortions. This highlights a new necessity for audio watermarking at this stage: it must be robust against audio synthesis models.
